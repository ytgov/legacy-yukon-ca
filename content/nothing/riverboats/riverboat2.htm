The Riverboat Era

Three Canadian Development Company sternwheelers – the Columbian, Canadian and Victorian – docked in Dawson, 1899. These three ships were built in Esquimalt, B.C., steamed up the coast of B.C. and Alaska to the mouth of the Yukon River, then upriver over 1600 km to Dawson City.

Yukon sternwheelers consumed immense quantities of wood; in 1915, the British Yukon Navigation Company fleet burned 8,000 cords of firewood. A cord is a stack of four-foot-long logs piled four feet high by eight feet long (1.2m x 1.2m x 2.4m). Every year, entire families moved out to work in the wood camps. In the 1930s woodcutters were paid $5 per cord, all cut with handsaws.
