The Riverboat Era

The early 1920s marked the arrival of the first aircraft in the Yukon. Soon airplanes became an important means of delivering passengers and freight to remote locations.

This historic photo shows the first airplane to land at Fort Selkirk on July 11, 1922. It was a Curtis Standard named the Polar Bear II. The pilot was attempting to fly from Niagara Falls, Ontario to Siberia but the flight ended in a crash just out of Eagle, Alaska. L-R: Sophie Anderson (Old Abraham’s daughter), Old Abraham, John MacMartin and Mrs. MacMartin.

YA, Van Bibber Family fonds, 79/2 #62, PHO 100.
The building of the Alaska Highway during World War II, followed by the postwar construction of other year-round roads, brought an end to the sternwheeler era in the early 1950s. The lack of a regular supply line meant the death of many Yukon River communities, such as Fort Selkirk.
