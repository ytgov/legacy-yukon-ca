The Alaska Highway

When World War II broke out, the battlefields of Europe seemed far from the Yukon. After the bombing of Pearl Harbour and the subsequent Japanese landings in the Aleutian Islands, however, an invasion of mainland North America seemed imminent. In early 1942, Canada and the United States agreed to build a 2400-km highway to Alaska through northeastern British Columbia and the Yukon, using American forces.

Tens of thousands of American troops and civilian workers poured into the area to work on defence projects. This “Friendly Invasion” caused many upheavals and changed the ways that people lived and travelled, even after the war had ended.
