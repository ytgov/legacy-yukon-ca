The Hän and the Gold Rush

The heart of their traditional territory was Tr’ochëk, a fish camp at the mouth of the Klondike River, only a short distance from the gold discovery on Bonanza Creek. Within a year, this site was overwhelmed by miners and others attracted by the gold discoveries.


Tr’ochëk, the Tr’ondëk Hwëch’in’ fish camp at the mouth of the Klondike River, in 1895.
W.D. Johns photographer. YA, Robert Coutts fonds, 82/358 #2, PHO 045.
Just a few months after the discovery of gold in 1896, the great rush of stampeding miners forced the Tr’ondëk Hwëch’in’ from their traditional fish camp at Tr’ochëk to a small settlement at Moosehide, a few km down the Yukon River from Dawson City.
Moosehide village, on the east bank of the Yukon River.
