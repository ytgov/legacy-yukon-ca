Sites of Interest

Council of Yukon First Nations. http://www.cyfn.ca/

Fort Selkirk. This Virtual Museum of Canada website features the history and culture of the Northern Tutchone people who lived in the Fort Selkirk area of the Yukon. http://www.virtualmuseum.ca/Exhibitions/FortSelkirk/english/index.html

The Bishop Who Ate His Boots. A Virtual Museum of Canada website highlighting the life and travels of Yukon Bishop Isaac O. Stringer. http://www.virtualmuseum.ca/Exhibitions/BishopStringer/english/index.html

Yukon Archives Images Database. This pilot project includes 1452 images from three Yukon Archives collections. This small sample of the extensive YA photo collections is searchable and includes many Klondike Gold Rush and First Nations (search using "Indian") images.
http://www.tc.gov.yk.ca/digitization/public/index.php

Yukon Beringia Interpretive Centre. http://www.beringia.com/

Yukon Government Heritage Resources Unit: http://www.yukonheritage.com/

Yukon Native Language Centre http://www.yukoncollege.yk.ca/ynlc/

The Yukon Photographers: The Gold Rush Era. To see more photographs of the great Klondike Gold Rush, visit this site.
http://www.virtualmuseum.ca/Exhibitions/Goldrushphoto/02english/02intro.html
