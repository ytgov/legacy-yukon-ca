Education

Education was a Canadian government responsibility, but it was the Church that handled First Nations schooling with some federal funding. In some communities, such as Fort Selkirk and Moosehide, missionaries set up day schools that operated when families were in town. Attendance was irregular and church officials felt they could exert a greater “civilizing” influence if students lived in residential schools, away from the influence of their families and community. This not only broke up families, but also deprived children of their culture.
