Introduction

The two institutions with the greatest influence on the lives of Yukon First Nations people were the Canadian government and the Church, particularly the Anglican Church. Missionaries spread the message of Christianity and, with some government funding, took on the task of First Nations education. The Yukon’s first Indian Agent, John Hawksley, was also an Anglican missionary. Although government officials encouraged First Nations people to continue earning a living by hunting, fishing and trapping, government regulations limited access to the land. Many individual clergy members and civil servants tried to improve the lives of First Nations people, although the policies they implemented often made life more difficult.

The Old Territorial Administration Building in Dawson City, completed in 1901, was an expression of confidence by the Canadian government in the future of the new Yukon Territory. This restored and renovated neoclassical building now houses the Dawson City Museum.

  


The Red Ensign was Canada's flag until 1965, when it was replaced by the current maple leaf design. It features the British Union Jack, in the top left corner, and the Canadian coat of arms.
