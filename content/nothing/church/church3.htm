The Church

Isaac O. Stringer became the Yukon’s second Anglican Bishop in 1905. Over the next 25 years, he travelled extensively to visit camps and settlements within his large diocese. On one of the few occasions that he did not take a First Nations guide, he and a companion were lost for several weeks in the north Yukon. In desperation, the two men subsisted on Stringer’s boiled and toasted rawhide sealskin boots. Stringer became famous as “the Bishop who ate his boots.”
