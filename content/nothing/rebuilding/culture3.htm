Yukon International Storytelling Festival
The festival was co-founded by Tagish elder Angela Sidney in 1988. Storytellers, dancers, musicians and performers come from all over the world to perform, many of them in their native languages. Yukon First Nations people share their lives and traditions by telling stories, drumming and dancing.

Charlie Peter Charlie
Gwich’in elder Charlie Peter Charlie taught himself to play the fiddle more than 60 years ago. For many years, he was lead fiddler at the International Athapaskan Fiddle Festival in Alaska. He is also a Gwich’in linguist and has been documenting his language and traditions. For 12 years, he served as chief of the Vuntut Gwitchin. He shared his skills in living on the land with his large family and advised and guided generations of anthropologists, archaeologists and other researchers. In 1988, at age 69, Charlie Peter Charlie was admitted to the Order of Canada in honour of his many years of service to the community of Old Crow, to the Gwich'in language and traditional knowledge, and to the Yukon as a whole.
