Yukon Native Language Centre
In the 1970s, Yukon elders began working with linguists to record their languages and put them into written form. The staff of the Yukon Native Language Centre researches the eight First Nations languages, trains teachers and develops teaching materials and programs for teaching native languages to everyone from preschoolers to elders.

Ayamdigut
Respected elder, storyteller and linguist, Angela Sidney, stands with Premier Tony Penikett and Education Minister Piers McDonald at the official opening of the new Yukon College campus in 1988. Mrs. Sidney gave the college a Tlingit name, “Ayamdigut,” meaning “she got up and went” referring to the relocation of the campus to its new home on a hill above downtown Whitehorse.
