People of the North

Travel & Transportation

People travelled light, carrying only the basic materials they needed for shelter, clothing, hunting and trapping. When resources were plentiful, as during summer salmon runs, people gathered in greater numbers at large camps. If food was scarce, they split up into small family groups.

This camp is near Champagne in the southern Yukon, 1898.

In summer, people crossed and travelled on rivers using everything from simple rafts to moosehide boats to elegant lightweight birchbark canoes.
