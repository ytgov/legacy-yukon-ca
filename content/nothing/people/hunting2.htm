People of the North

Hunting & Fishing

These salmon traps, at Klukshu in the southern Yukon, were built using methods learned from the Coastal Tlingit. Salmon could easily swim into the structure, but a row of stakes at the entrance prevented their escape.


 


YA, Richard Harrington fonds, 85/25 #301, PHO 278.

Fish wheels were developed on the lower Yukon River about 1904. The river current turned the wheel and paddles scooped up the fish, which were funnelled into “cruise boxes” on either side of the wheel.
