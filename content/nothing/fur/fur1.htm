Introduction

Yukon people relied on trade with other First Nations to obtain goods that were unavailable in their area. In the early days, a thriving trade existed between aboriginal people of the interior and those in the coastal areas. Trade patterns changed with the arrival of Russian, British and American traders. Trapping for fur continued to be an important source of income in the 20th century, although drops in fur prices and government regulations that limited access to the land made it harder for people to earn a living this way.
