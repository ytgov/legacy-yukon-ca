Yukon Women Work Hard
 
Then, as now, Yukon mothers wore many hats: nurse, teacher, barber, chef and tailor. The women who raised their children in the Yukon wilderness had to be on constant alert. The adventurous young toddler faced many potential hazards: swarms of biting insects, winter temperatures of minus forty degrees, swift, freezing rivers and even encounters with bears. Even so, most children thrived in the healthy outdoor environment and learned independence and self-reliance.
