Birth & Infancy
Sarah Ann “Sadie” StringerSadie Stringer was a missionary living on Herschel Island, an Inuit settlement off the Yukon’s north coast, when she gave birth to her second child in 1900. Her only support came from her husband; Reverend Isaac Stringer helped Sadie deliver a healthy boy.

August 30, 1903
"Dear mother and brothers and all inquiring friends …"

“You will have heard long before this of the birth of our dear little son Freddie Herschel, on the 25th of May 1900. It was earlier than I expected but it was my own fault as I insisted on helping with some wall-papering on the 23rd and I feel sure that is what brought it on so early. However five hours from 5 a.m. till 10 a.m. Isaac was the only person with me through all. God was with us both though and carried us safely through all. I feel that God is especially near to us here at such times, cut off as we are from all human aid.”

Excerpted from a letter to Mrs. F. G. Newton. YA, Isaac and Sadie Stringer fonds, 82/16, MF#53.
