Outstanding Yukon Women!
Dereen Hildebrand
1952 – 2004
Dereen Hildebrand was one of the Yukon's most visible artists. As well as creating fine art in a variety of media, she decorated the windows of local businesses with lively colourful images. Her specialised skill of writing backwards was put to good use: her designs and messages marked a variety of occasions from Christmas to Sourdough Rendezvous, the annual Whitehorse winter carnival. Dereen also founded the popular summer Arts in the Park program. Yukon performers and visual artists gather at LePage Park in Whitehorse to enliven downtown lunch hours. This dynamic artist and community builder has been greatly missed since her sudden death in January 2004.

Gail Lotenberg

Gail Lotenberg’s life has been shaped by dance. Through her work, she has shared her vision of the north with audiences all over Canada and the United States. After moving to the Yukon in 1993, Gail began to see movement and metaphor in the wild and vast world that surrounded her. Gail was inspired by the physical and cultural landscape of the North to found LINK Dance Company in 2000. LINK Dance Company is Canada’s first professional contemporary dance company north of the 60th parallel.
