Outstanding Yukon Women!
Martha Ballentine Cameron
1904 – 1990

Martha Ballentine was born and raised in Dawson City, where she met her future husband, G.I. or “Cam” Cameron. Cameron had to leave the RCMP in order to marry – a regulation in those days – but in 1934 he rejoined the force and the couple ran the detachment at Fort Selkirk for the next 15 years. Martha led an active, busy life. As well as raising her daughter and running her log cabin household, she took on many extra jobs. She cut the detachment’s firewood (before there were power saws!), maintained the local airstrip, acted as agent for the airline (which meant handling all freight and mail), and dispensed medicine for the Department of Indian Affairs. She even had her own radio show: once a week she went on the air using the Camerons’ two-way radio to read the Dawson City newspaper to trappers in their remote cabins. Throughout her life, this energetic and generous woman was an inspiration to all who knew her.
