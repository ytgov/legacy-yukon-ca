Outstanding Yukon Women!
Martha Benjamin

In 1963, 25-year-old Martha Benjamin became the first Yukoner to win a national cross-country skiing title. During the late 1950s and early 1960s, Mrs. Benjamin competed in Canada, the U.S. and Europe. The Old Crow resident and mother of five won the senior women’s championships in Midland, Ontario, winning the ten-km race. In 1989, Mrs. Benjamin was inducted into the Yukon Sports Hall of Fame.

Jeane Lassen

This determined young woman began lifting weights when she was 12 years old. For most of the past ten years, she has been working toward her goal of representing Canada in the Olympics. In 1995, after winning silver at the Canada Winter Games at age 14, she received the Roland Michener Award, presented to young Canada Games participants who exemplify leadership, cooperation and achievement. She has attended championships all over the world and amassed an impressive stock of medals, including a bronze in the Pan American Championships in 1999. When she was 18, Lassen held the Canadian record for two different lifts. Sport Yukon has named her female athlete of the year no less than four times.
