Outstanding Yukon Women!
Babe Southwick
1925 – 1965

Babe Southwick was one of the best-known mushers in the sled dog races at the Sourdough Rendezvous winter carnival. A member of the Dickson family in the Kluane Lake area, Babe was a mother of four and a colourful member of the local dog-mushing scene. It was a great shock when she died, apparently from a sudden heart attack, only hours after a race. Her racing bib number was retired and the Babe Southwick Memorial Trophy, inspired by her “great spirit and high reputation,” is awarded to the team with the fastest lap of the three heats.
