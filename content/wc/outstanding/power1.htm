Martha Louise Black
1866 – 1957

Martha Louise Black was elected as the Yukon’s Member of Parliament in 1935, the first woman to represent the territory nationally. This gold rush pioneer was also the first naturalized Canadian to be elected to Parliament. An indomitable woman, she brought valuable experience and many talents to the job. During her long life, she was a mother, businesswoman, hostess of the Commissioner’s Residence, war worker, lecturer, author, botanist and wife of the Speaker of the Canadian House of Commons.

When Martha’s husband, George Black, and her son Lyman both enlisted to fight in Europe during World War I, Martha joined them on the troop ship overseas. During the war, she was based in London and kept busy with volunteer war work, giving lectures about the Yukon, writing newspaper articles and visiting wounded soldiers.
