The board of directors and executive members of Yukon Indian Women’s Association (YIWA), ca. 1977. YIWA was established in 1974 to provide First Nations’ women with an organized voice to address issues affecting their lives.

Kaushee Harris

Kaushee Harris, a Taku River Tlingit woman of the Crow Clan, overcame many challenges in her life to provide for her family of seven children. She was the first president of the Yukon Indian Women’s Association and worked hard to establish a safe place for Yukon women and children in distress. In 1980, a year after her death, the first Women’s Transition Home opened in Whitehorse; it was named Kaushee’s Place in her honour. When a larger building opened in 1992, the opening ceremony was attended by Kaushee’s children, her seven brothers and sisters and her mother, Tlingit elder Antonia Jack.
